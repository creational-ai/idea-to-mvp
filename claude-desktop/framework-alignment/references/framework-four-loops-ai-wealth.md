## Success Indicators

According to this framework, you're doing it right when:

1. **Balance Loop:**
   - You can clearly articulate your asymmetric advantage
   - You've identified specific, urgent, frequent, painful customer frustrations
   - You're testing assumptions in hours/days, not months
   - You're adjusting balance constantly (not fixed on one approach)

2. **Speed to Revenue Loop:**
   - You're shipping frequently (daily or weekly, not quarterly)
   - You're using your own product to build your product
   - You treat PMF as a moving target, not a destination
   - You're learning in real time, not planning 18 months out

3. **Signal to Innovation Loop:**
   - You have clear signal sources from actual user behavior
   - You look at signals frequently (daily, not monthly)
   - Your product decisions come from data, not assumptions about what users "should" want
   - You're building feedback mechanisms that compound

4. **Sweat Equity Loop:**
   - You have deep conviction in what you're building
   - You're in the details, not delegating and disappearing
   - You treat setbacks as learning (the leaf feeding the soil)
   - You're focused on who you're becoming, not just what you're building

---

## Warning Signs

You're off-track when:

1. **Balance Loop failures:**
   - Building a "clever product nobody urgently needs"
   - Chasing hot markets where you have no real advantage
   - Making big assumptions without rapid testing
   - Static positioning (not adjusting balance)
   - Becoming a "generic me-too company" without your own lane

2. **Speed to Revenue Loop failures:**
   - Quarterly roadmaps and 6-9 month release cycles
   - Planning 18 months ahead in AI
   - Not using your own product
   - Treating PMF as a one-time achievement

3. **Signal to Innovation Loop failures:**
   - Defending your original idea instead of listening to data
   - Asking "what do we think users will like?" instead of "what are they actually doing?"
   - Big fixed ideas about how people should behave
   - No feedback mechanisms built into product

4. **Sweat Equity Loop failures:**
   - Delegating and disappearing too early
   - Lacking deep conviction (doing it for money alone)
   - Giving up when others would (the 99%)
   - Not sweating the details

## Validation Questions

Use these to check any project against this framework:

### Balance Loop
- [ ] Can I articulate my asymmetric advantage in one sentence?
- [ ] What's my unfair edge vs. other startups and incumbents?
- [ ] Have I identified a pain that is urgent, frequent, AND painful?
- [ ] Am I chasing a billion-dollar market or fixing thousand-dollar frustrations that happen a million times?
- [ ] What are my top 3 assumptions right now?
- [ ] What's the fastest way I can test each assumption?
- [ ] When did I last adjust my balance between advantage and pain?
